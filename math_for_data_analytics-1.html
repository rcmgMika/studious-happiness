01 — Foundation

Pre-Algebra & Algebra

The bedrock of all quantitative reasoning in analytics.
Pre-Algebra Foundational
  • Order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)
  • Integer & rational number arithmetic
  • Fractions, decimals, percentages
  • Ratios and proportions
  • Absolute value
  • Factoring integers & prime factorization
  • GCF and LCM
  • Exponents and roots (including fractional exponents)
  • Scientific notation
Algebra I Core
  • Variables, expressions, and equations
  • Solving linear equations & inequalities
  • Systems of linear equations
  • Polynomial operations & factoring
  • Quadratic equations (factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula)
  • Functions: definition, notation, domain, range
  • Slope and linear functions
Algebra II Core
  • Exponential and logarithmic functions
  • Laws of logarithms (product, quotient, power)
  • Natural logarithm (ln) and base-e
  • Rational functions & expressions
  • Sequences and series (arithmetic & geometric)
  • Sigma notation (Σ)
  • Binomial theorem
  • Complex numbers (basics)
Functions & Graphs Core
  • Composition and inverse functions
  • Piecewise functions
  • Transformations (shift, scale, reflect)
  • Modeling with functions
  • Polynomial and power functions
  • Absolute value functions
  • Step functions

02 — Core Analytics

Statistics & Probability

The language of data — central to every analytics workflow.
Descriptive Statistics Essential
  • Mean, median, mode, midrange
  • Variance and standard deviation (population vs sample)
  • Range, IQR, quartiles, percentiles
  • Skewness and kurtosis
  • Covariance and correlation (Pearson, Spearman, Kendall)
  • Five-number summary & box plots
  • Z-scores and standardization
  • Frequency distributions and histograms
Probability Theory Essential
  • Sample spaces, events, outcomes
  • Axioms of probability
  • Classical, frequentist, Bayesian interpretations
  • Addition and multiplication rules
  • Conditional probability: P(A|B)
  • Bayes' Theorem
  • Independence vs. mutual exclusivity
  • Permutations and combinations
  • Law of total probability
Probability Distributions Essential
  • Discrete: Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric
  • Continuous: Uniform, Normal (Gaussian), Exponential
  • Student's t-distribution
  • Chi-squared (χ²) distribution
  • F-distribution
  • Beta and Gamma distributions
  • PDF, CDF, PMF — definitions and usage
  • Expected value E[X] and variance Var[X]
  • Moment generating functions
Inferential Statistics Essential
  • Sampling distributions & Central Limit Theorem
  • Point estimates and confidence intervals
  • Hypothesis testing framework (H₀ vs Hₐ)
  • p-values and significance levels (α)
  • Type I and Type II errors
  • z-test, t-test (one-sample, two-sample, paired)
  • ANOVA (one-way and two-way)
  • Chi-squared tests (goodness of fit, independence)
  • Power of a test
Bayesian Statistics Advanced
  • Prior, likelihood, posterior distributions
  • Bayesian updating
  • Conjugate priors
  • Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation
  • Credible intervals
  • Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) — overview
Regression Analysis Essential
  • Simple linear regression (OLS)
  • Multiple linear regression
  • Assumptions: linearity, normality, homoscedasticity, independence
  • R², Adjusted R², RMSE, MAE
  • Residuals analysis
  • Logistic regression (log-odds, sigmoid)
  • Polynomial regression
  • Ridge (L2), Lasso (L1), Elastic Net regularization

03 — Core Analytics

Linear Algebra

The mathematics of data tables, transformations, and dimensionality.
Vectors Essential
  • Vector notation and geometric interpretation
  • Vector addition and scalar multiplication
  • Dot product (inner product)
  • Cross product
  • Vector norms (L1, L2, L∞)
  • Unit vectors and normalization
  • Orthogonality and orthonormality
  • Projection of vectors
  • Linear independence
Matrices Essential
  • Matrix addition, subtraction, scalar multiplication
  • Matrix multiplication (row × column rule)
  • Transpose (Aᵀ)
  • Identity and zero matrices
  • Symmetric, diagonal, orthogonal matrices
  • Matrix inverse and conditions for invertibility
  • Determinants and their properties
  • Trace of a matrix
  • Rank of a matrix
Systems & Decompositions Essential
  • Solving Ax = b (Gaussian elimination, back-substitution)
  • Row echelon form, reduced row echelon form
  • LU decomposition
  • QR decomposition
  • Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)
  • Cholesky decomposition
  • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
  • Eigendecomposition
  • Null space and column space
Vector Spaces & Transformations Advanced
  • Span, basis, and dimension
  • Linear transformations as matrices
  • Change of basis
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) — math
  • Positive definite matrices
  • Covariance matrices
  • Kernel methods (inner product spaces)

04 — Core Analytics

Calculus

Optimization, rates of change, and the math behind learning algorithms.
Differential Calculus Core
  • Limits and continuity
  • Definition of the derivative
  • Differentiation rules: power, product, quotient, chain
  • Derivatives of: polynomials, exponentials, logarithms, trig
  • Higher-order derivatives
  • Implicit differentiation
  • Critical points, local/global minima and maxima
  • Concavity, inflection points
  • L'Hôpital's Rule
Integral Calculus Core
  • Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
  • Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  • Definite integrals and area under curves
  • Integration techniques: substitution, by parts
  • Improper integrals
  • Numerical integration (trapezoidal, Simpson's rule)
  • Probability density functions (continuous distributions)
Multivariable Calculus Advanced
  • Partial derivatives (∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y)
  • Gradient vector (∇f)
  • Directional derivatives
  • The Jacobian matrix
  • The Hessian matrix (second-order partial derivatives)
  • Chain rule for multivariable functions
  • Taylor series expansion (multivariate)
  • Lagrange multipliers for constrained optimization
Optimization (Calculus-Based) Essential
  • Gradient Descent (batch, stochastic, mini-batch)
  • Learning rate and convergence
  • Momentum, Adam, RMSprop (conceptual math)
  • Convex vs. non-convex functions
  • Saddle points and local minima
  • Cost / loss function minimization
  • Second-order optimization (Newton's method)

05 — Supporting

Discrete Mathematics

Logic, sets, graphs — essential for databases, algorithms, and data structures.
Set Theory Core
  • Sets, subsets, power sets, universal set
  • Union (∪), intersection (∩), difference, complement
  • Cartesian products
  • Venn diagrams
  • Cardinality (finite and infinite sets)
  • Relations: reflexive, symmetric, transitive
  • Functions as relations
Logic & Proofs Core
  • Propositional logic (AND, OR, NOT, XOR)
  • Truth tables
  • Implications, biconditionals
  • Logical equivalence, De Morgan's Laws
  • Predicate logic: quantifiers (∀, ∃)
  • Proof techniques: direct, contrapositive, contradiction, induction
Combinatorics Core
  • Fundamental counting principle
  • Permutations (with and without repetition)
  • Combinations (nCr)
  • Pascal's triangle
  • Inclusion-exclusion principle
  • Pigeonhole principle
  • Generating functions (basics)
Graph Theory Advanced
  • Graphs, vertices, edges, directed/undirected
  • Adjacency matrices and lists
  • Paths, cycles, trees
  • Connectivity, degree
  • Bipartite graphs
  • Shortest path (Dijkstra)
  • Network flows and PageRank

07 — Advanced

Information Theory & Optimization

Mathematical foundations of feature selection, model evaluation, and loss functions.
Information Theory Advanced
  • Entropy H(X) = -Σ p(x) log p(x)
  • Joint entropy and conditional entropy
  • Mutual information I(X;Y)
  • Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence
  • Cross-entropy loss
  • Jensen-Shannon divergence
  • Information gain (used in decision trees)
  • Minimum description length
Mathematical Optimization Advanced
  • Convex sets and convex functions
  • Optimality conditions (KKT conditions)
  • Constrained optimization (Lagrangian method)
  • Linear programming (simplex method)
  • Quadratic programming
  • Dual problems and strong duality
  • Subgradients for non-smooth functions
  • Proximal methods (ADMM)
Time Series Mathematics Applied
  • Stationarity and unit roots
  • Autocorrelation (ACF) and partial autocorrelation (PACF)
  • Autocovariance function
  • ARIMA model formulation
  • Fourier transforms and frequency domain
  • Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT)
  • Exponential smoothing (Holt-Winters)
  • Spectral analysis

08 — Applied ML

Machine Learning Mathematics

The unified mathematics underpinning modern ML and data science models.
Loss Functions & Metrics Essential
  • Mean Squared Error (MSE), MAE, RMSE
  • Cross-entropy (binary & categorical)
  • Hinge loss (SVM)
  • Huber loss
  • Log-likelihood and MLE
  • Precision, Recall, F1, AUC-ROC
  • Confusion matrix mathematics
Dimensionality Reduction Essential
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) — full derivation
  • Singular Value Decomposition in PCA
  • Explained variance ratio
  • t-SNE mathematics (Kullback-Leibler)
  • UMAP (topology and fuzzy sets)
  • Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA)
  • Factor analysis
Probabilistic Models Advanced
  • Naive Bayes classifier mathematics
  • Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM) — EM algorithm
  • Hidden Markov Models (HMM)
  • Expectation-Maximization (E-step, M-step)
  • Variational inference basics
  • Gaussian processes
Neural Network Math Advanced
  • Feedforward propagation (matrix form)
  • Activation functions and derivatives: ReLU, sigmoid, tanh, softmax
  • Backpropagation via chain rule
  • Vanishing/exploding gradients
  • Weight initialization strategies
  • Batch normalization mathematics
  • Dropout as regularization
  • Attention mechanism: Q, K, V matrices
Kernel Methods & SVMs Advanced
  • Margin maximization (SVM primal)
  • Dual formulation and support vectors
  • Kernel trick: k(x,x') = φ(x)·φ(x')
  • Linear, polynomial, RBF kernels
  • Kernel matrix (Gram matrix)
  • Mercer's theorem
Decision Trees & Ensemble Math Essential
  • Gini impurity: 1 - Σpᵢ²
  • Information gain (entropy-based splitting)
  • Variance reduction (regression trees)
  • Bagging and bootstrap aggregation
  • Bias-variance decomposition
  • AdaBoost weight updates
  • Gradient Boosting — additive model math
