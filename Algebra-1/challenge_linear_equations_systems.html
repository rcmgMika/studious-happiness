Subtopic 01 · 5 Problems

Solving Linear Equations & Inequalities

Isolate the variable. Maintain balance. Handle inequalities with care around negatives.
Quick Reminders Before You Start
Whatever you do to one side, do to the other.
Distribute before collecting like terms.
When multiplying or dividing an inequality by a negative number, flip the inequality sign.
Always verify your equation answer by substituting back.
For inequalities, express the solution as an interval or inequality, not just a number.

Subtopic 02 · 5 Problems

Systems of Linear Equations

Two or more equations, two or more unknowns. Find the values that satisfy all equations simultaneously.
Quick Reminders Before You Start
Substitution: isolate one variable in one equation, substitute into the other.
Elimination: multiply equations to match one variable's coefficient, then add or subtract to cancel it.
Always solve for BOTH variables — not just the first one you find.
Verify by substituting your (x, y) into BOTH original equations.
No solution → parallel lines. Infinite solutions → same line.

Word problem — build a system and solve it
Systems · Real-world modeling · Data context
Hard
Problem
A company runs two types of ad campaigns — digital ads and print ads.

In Week 1, they ran 5 digital and 3 print ads, generating 1,340 total clicks.
In Week 2, they ran 2 digital and 7 print ads, generating 1,020 total clicks.

Assuming each digital ad generates d clicks and each print ad generates p clicks consistently, find the average clicks per digital ad and per print ad.
Set up a system of two equations with two unknowns (d and p), then solve. Interpret your answer in a sentence.
💡
Hint: Equation 1 comes from Week 1's data: 5d + 3p = 1340. Write Equation 2 from Week 2's data. Then solve the system using substitution or elimination. After solving, write a sentence stating what each value means.
✏️
Write both equations, your full solution steps, and a sentence interpreting d and p, then share it.
